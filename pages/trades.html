Electrician

Course Overview

Our Electrician trade program is a flagship vocational course offering rich, hands-on exposure to both domestic and heavy industrial electrical systems. Trainees learn safety precautions, electrical installation design, transformer winding, domestic panel setups, and power transmission grids. Our lab is equipped with modern training boards, generators, and testing equipment to ensure every student gains critical competencies.

Curriculum Highlights

  • AC/DC Machine Winding & Armature servicing
  • High tension & low tension cable jointing
  • Industrial control panel boards designing & wiring
  • Transformer oil testing and maintenance
  • Earthing setups (plate & pipe) and solar panel installations

Career Opportunities

Graduates find highly lucrative employment opportunities in electricity generation and distribution companies (such as KSEB, NTPC, NPCIL), Indian Railways, Metro services, large manufacturing complexes, marine repair docks, and as licensed electrical supervisors or independent contractors.

Quick Trade Facts

  • AccreditationNCVT affiliated
  • Duration2 Years (4 Semesters)
  • Seats Capacity80 Seats
  • EligibilitySSLC / 10th Pass
  • CertificationNational Trade Cert (NTC)

Draughtsman Civil

Course Overview

Draughtsman Civil provides comprehensive training in architectural design, leveling surveying, municipal building layouts, and modern CAD software. Trainees are taught the science of engineering drawing, map generation, structural detailing, estimating, and cost valuation. A major highlight is our computer-aided drafting lab, where students master industry-standard software to build 2D and 3D mockups.

Curriculum Highlights

  • 2D and 3D architectural drawing using AutoCAD
  • Leveling surveys with Total Station and Auto-level
  • Building estimations, costing, and municipal regulatory guidelines
  • RCC structural detailing and foundation designs
  • Topographic surveying and landscape mapping

Career Opportunities

Civil Draughtsmen are in high demand in construction companies, land surveying agencies, real estate developers, municipal corporations, PWD, and consulting engineering groups. Many graduates start their own visual blueprint design practices.

Quick Trade Facts

  • Duration2 Years (4 Semesters)

Electronic Mechanic

Course Overview

The Electronic Mechanic course introduces trainees to active/passive components, microcontrollers, digital logic designs, computer hardware troubleshooting, and communication devices. Students work extensively on soldering, PCB circuit routing, sensor interfaces, and diagnostics of industrial electronic systems. The program equips students with modern testing tools such as digital oscilloscopes, function generators, and spectrum analyzers.

Curriculum Highlights

  • SMD component soldering and PCB fabrication
  • Microcontroller programming (8051, PIC, Arduino)
  • Digital display systems, fiber optics, and sensor interfaces
  • SMPS, UPS, and inverter circuit troubleshooting
  • Mobile communication hardware and computer networking basics

Career Opportunities

Our graduates find employment in electronics production corporations (such as Keltron, BEL, ISRO, and private IoT firms), mobile networks, hardware diagnostics centers, solar cell installation networks, and in product servicing enterprises globally.

Quick Trade Facts

  • AccreditationNCVT affiliated
  • Duration2 Years (4 Semesters)
  • Seats Capacity40 Seats
  • EligibilitySSLC / 10th Pass
  • CertificationNational Trade Cert (NTC)

Wireman

Course Overview

Our Wireman trade course prepares trainees to work with high/low voltage wiring grids, overhead transmission lines, transformer maintenance, and motor setups. Focus is given to commercial power boards, internal panel routings, street lighting controls, and safety equipment handling. Regular outdoor training makes wireman trainees highly practical and competent for power line distribution grids.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Domestic, commercial, and industrial wiring layouts
  • Overhead line construction, insulators, and jointing
  • AC/DC motor installations and control starters
  • Cable jointing and panel boards construction
  • Earth testing and electrical safety codes

Career Opportunities

Wireman graduates have excellent demand in power corporations (KSEB, state electricity departments), construction projects, municipal corporations, public lighting cells, and as licensed wiring supervisors.

Quick Trade Facts

  • AccreditationNCVT affiliated
  • Duration2 Years (4 Semesters)
  • Seats Capacity40 Seats

NCVT Code 443

Plumber

Course Overview

The Plumber trade course is a practical one-year vocational program focusing on domestic and commercial plumbing infrastructure. Trainees learn to layout pipeline systems, perform GI and PVC jointing, install high-quality sanitary fixtures, and maintain booster pumps. Trainees also receive training on solar water heaters, modern drain fittings, and water filter connections.

Curriculum Highlights

  • Blueprints reading and piping layouts
  • GI, PVC, Copper, and PPR pipe jointing techniques
  • Booster pump connection and automatic float systems
  • Sanitary fittings and drainage pipe routing
  • Modern hot water solar structures installation

Career Opportunities

Plumbing professionals are in constant demand in municipal corporations, water authority networks, large high-rise housing maintenance divisions, construction contractors, and as independent contractors in India and the Gulf region.

Quick Trade Facts

  • AccreditationNCVT affiliated
  • Duration1 Year (2 Semesters)
  • Seats Capacity20 Seats
  • Eligibility8th / 10th Pass
  • CertificationNational Trade Cert (NTC)
